<p>The NCERT Physics textbooks are the official resources prescribed by the NCERT for senior secondary education. The books are designed to build a strong foundation in core concepts such as mechanics, electricity, magnetism, optics, and modern physics through clear explanations and logical progression.</p>


<ul>
        <li>Physics is divided into two <b>parts</b>: Part 1 and Part 2.


        <ul>
                <li><b>Part 1</b>: Chapters from 1 to 8 are included in this part.</li>
                <li><b>Part 2</b>: Chapters from 9 to 14 are included in this part</li>
        </ul>
        </li>
        <li>The total number of <b>units is 9</b>, which includes <b>14 chapters</b>. A chapter on <b>Communication systems</b> has been <b>deleted</b>.</li>
        <li>The content strictly follows the syllabus recommended for CBSE and other major boards, making it an essential resource for board exam preparation.</li>
        <li>NCERT Physics textbooks have a significant weightage of <b>70-80%</b> and <b>60-70%</b> in <b>NEET </b>and <b>JEE Main</b> exams, respectively.</li>
</ul>


<p>Almost 100% of CBSE board exam questions are based on the NCERT concepts, examples, and exercises.</p>

<h2>Pro Tips to cover NCERT Class 12th Physics</h2>


<p>The tips to cover the NCERT class 12th Physics are based on the toppers' advice and teacher’s guidance. Your daily study plan should include theory, numericals and the PYQs.</p>


<p><b>Tips to learn faster</b></p>


<ul>
        <li>You have to study Physics daily</li>
        <li>Solve numericals along with theory</li>
        <li>You should revise the formulas every week</li>
        <li>Make a formula sheet to quickly revise formulas</li>
        <li>You must solve the Class 12th physics PYQs</li>
</ul>


<p><b>How toppers study Physics</b></p>


<ul>
        <li>Concept clarity should be the aim</li>
        <li>PYQs should be solved along with the NCERT reading</li>
        <li>Revision is the key</li>
</ul>


<p><i>You have to focus on revising and practicing what you have studied, and not on increasing your resources.</i></p>
